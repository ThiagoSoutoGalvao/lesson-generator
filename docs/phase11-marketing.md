# Phase 11 — Marketing Strategy & Revenue Targets

## Validate Before Building

Before writing any payment code, message your 4 beta users individually:
> *"I'm thinking of charging $12/month for full access. Would you pay that?"*

- 2+ say yes → build the payment system immediately
- All say no → ask why, fix the product issue first

---

## Getting the First Strangers to Pay

Do these in order:

**Week 1 — Make a 60-second screen recording**
Record: open app, upload a page, type prompt, watch activity generate. No voice needed — screen + upbeat music. Use this asset everywhere.

**Week 1 — Post in Facebook Groups**
Groups: "TEFL teachers", "English teachers abroad", "Online ESL teachers", "EFL teachers" (10k–100k members each).
Caption: *"I built a tool that generates classroom activities from any course book in seconds. Free to try — would love feedback."*
Lead with demo, ask for feedback — no sales pitch.

**Week 1 — Post on Reddit**
- r/TEFL (~80k members)
- r/languagelearning (~2M members)
- r/Teachers

**Ongoing — Instagram/TikTok Reels**
Format: *"POV: you need 5 activities before your next class"* → generate in 10 seconds. Post 3×/week for a month.

**Week 2 — Teachers Pay Teachers / Tes**
Upload a free resource ("10 free ESL activities for Speak Out B1"). Mention Lesson Generator in the description. High-intent buyers browse these platforms.

**Month 2 — Cold email language schools**
Search "online English school", "online TEFL academy". Email the director:
> *"I run a tool that lets teachers generate classroom activities from course books in seconds — cuts prep from 30 minutes to 30 seconds. Open to a free trial for your teachers?"*
Target 20 schools/week. 5% response rate = leads.

**Month 2 — Find one TEFL YouTuber or blogger**
Creators with 5k–50k subscribers. Offer 3 free months of Pro for an honest review. One trusted recommendation beats 100 ads.

**Month 3 — Product Hunt launch**
Prepare description, screenshots, demo video. Launch Tuesday or Wednesday (highest traffic). Ask network to upvote.

## On Copycats

Copycats only show up when there is money to copy. By that point you'll have:
- An established user base with saved activities (switching cost — they lose their library)
- Prompt templates refined over hundreds of real classroom uses
- A brand teachers already trust

The real moat is iteration speed and user trust. Keep shipping improvements based on what users ask for.
